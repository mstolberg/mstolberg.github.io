August 31, 2020

Wine Prediction

For this study, we will explore how binary logistic regression yields insight into the physicochemical composition unique to red and white wine. We intend to show through our analysis a detailed review of the data provided, a predictive model relationship for categorization of wine (red vs. white) as it relates to the given variables, and the reproductive process for a regression model that would allow a statistical model to predict the wine category.
August 24, 2020

Predicting Attrition - A Driver for Creating Value, Realizing Strategy, and Refining key HR Processes

In a knowledge driven economy, people or human capital are emerging as the key competitive differentiator in today’s “war for talent”. Understanding the drivers of attrition are crucial to address underlying organizational issues and develop remediating strategies as Human Capital” comprises 70 -90% of an organization’s cost. In this study, we provide a framework for maximizing a retention strategy’s ROI by leveraging insights derived from predicting to prioritizing retention investments. We will leverage established machine learning techniques to identify which employees are at risk of leaving and determine the key factors that drive their decision and use the results to maximize retention investment value by strategically managing attrition and optimizing costs.
June 23, 2020

Predicting Seasonal Influenza - Forecasting with SARIMA

The Influenza Division at the Centers for Disease Control seeks to predict the traits for influenza in the U.S. which varies greatly from season to season.  Models for influenza are used for medical preparedness as well as scientific research.  For this case study, we utilized the seasonal autoregressive integrated moving average (SARIMA) model to predict the number of new influenza cases reported each week consecutively for 13 weeks (equivalent to 1 business quarter) beginning April 1, 2020.
